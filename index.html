About the Course

Instructor: Sarah Oberbichler

This course offers an introduction to Natural Language Processing (NLP) and its application in the humanities and cultural studies. Participants work with digitized newspaper collections from the German Digital Library and examine the topic of "Natural and Environmental Disasters in Media". Both theoretical foundations and practical applications of NLP methods are taught.

Course Content and Methodology:

  • Practical Application: Students learn to apply NLP tools to specific research questions. The digitized newspaper collections of the German Digital Library are used as a data basis, and modern analysis methods are employed.
  • Thematic Focus: The course focuses on the examination of natural and environmental disasters in media. It analyzes how these events are presented and discussed in historical and current media reports.
  • Interdisciplinary Approaches: The course explores how NLP technologies can open up new perspectives on cultural, historical, and social issues. It also reflects on how these methods complement and extend traditional humanities approaches.

Learning Objectives:

  • Application of relevant Python packages for NLP tasks on own research data
  • Preparation and structuring of large datasets for analysis
  • Use of transformer models and large language models for NLP tasks with extensive data volumes
  • Critical reflection on various methods (methodology critique)
  • Writing a scientific paper on the research results

Modules and Workloads

Module 1

Introduction to the topic, the course, and NLP • Introduction to Colab Notebooks • Python Crash Course 1

Natural Language Processing (NLP) stands at the intersection of linguistics, computer science, and artificial intelligence. As a rapidly evolving field, NLP has become increasingly crucial in our data-driven world, enabling machines to contextualize and generate human language in valuable and meaningful ways.

  • Core linguistic concepts relevant to NLP
  • Basic NLP tasks and their applications
  • Current challenges and future directions in the field

The course is designed to balance theoretical foundations with practical applications, ensuring students gain both conceptual understanding and hands-on experience.

To facilitate practical learning, this course utilizes Google Colab Notebooks. Colab is a cloud-based Jupyter notebook environment that requires no setup and provides free access to computing resources, including GPUs. Key features include:

  • Seamless integration with Google Drive for storage and sharing
  • Pre-installed libraries commonly used in NLP and machine learning
  • Collaborative editing and real-time sharing capabilities

As programming language we will use Python. Python has emerged as the de facto language for NLP and machine learning due to its simplicity, readability, and robust ecosystem of libraries. This module includes a crash course in Python, covering:

  • Basic syntax and data types
  • Control structures (if statements, loops)
  • Data structures (lists, dictionaries)

    • Conclusion

    This introductory module sets the foundation for our exploration of Natural Language Processing. By combining theoretical knowledge with practical Python skills and utilizing Colab Notebooks, students will be well-equipped to delve into the fascinating world of NLP, understanding its potential to revolutionize human-computer interaction and unlock insights from textual data.

    As we progress through the course, we will build upon these fundamentals, gradually introducing more complex concepts and techniques that form the cutting edge of NLP research and applications.
